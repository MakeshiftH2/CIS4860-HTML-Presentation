IT Procurement & Governance

How governments buy and manage technology — simply explained

Prepared for Mohammad Ansari

What is IT Procurement & Governance?

  • The study of how governments buy and use technology.
  • Main goal: spend public money effectively and efficiently.
  • Includes hardware, software, cloud services, and IT support.

What is IT Procurement?

  • Process of acquiring IT goods and services for government use.
  • Examples: computers, networks, cloud, data storage, IT consulting.
  • Guided by rules and regulations to prevent waste and corruption.

What is IT Governance?

  • Control, accountability, and rules in IT decision‑making.
  • Aligns IT spending with public goals and outcomes.
  • Manages risks, security, and compliance.

Why It Matters

  • Governments spend billions on IT — mistakes are costly.
  • Balance speed vs. accountability (tech moves fast, rules move slow).
  • Protects citizen data and security; builds public trust.

Key Steps in IT Procurement

  • Plan – Identify needs.
  • Research – Check market and vendors.
  • Define Requirements – Write what is needed.
  • Solicit Bids – RFPs, tenders, or contracts.
  • Evaluate & Award – Pick the best vendor.
  • Manage Contract – Monitor performance and compliance.

Challenges in IT Procurement/Governance

  • Complex rules — many laws and policies.
  • Cybersecurity risks — systems hold sensitive data.
  • Vendor lock‑in — hard to switch providers later.
  • Budget & delays — long approval processes.

Why Good IT Governance Helps

  • Saves taxpayer money.
  • Ensures fair competition among vendors.
  • Improves public services and innovation.
  • Reduces risks of corruption, waste, and failure.

IT Governance Components

Combination of business strategy and governance components.

  • Business Continuity
  • Financial Accountability
  • Regulatory Compliance
  • Risk Management
Source

Governance Frameworks

  • ITIL: Best practices for IT service management.
  • ITSM: Planning, implementing, managing IT services for user and business needs.
Source

Qualities of Governance Frameworks

  • Cybersecurity: Protecting data, HIPAA, personal info.
  • Disaster Recovery: Plans for data and system recovery.
Source

Daily IT Governance Responsibilities

  • Set objectives for IT with strategic planning.
  • Design and use an IT governance framework aligned with company goals.
  • Ensure ongoing compliance with laws and standards.
